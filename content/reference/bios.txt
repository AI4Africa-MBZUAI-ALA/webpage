 



  
Professor Souhaib Ben Taieb 
Souhaib Ben Taieb is an Associate Professor and Associate Department Chair of Statistics and Data Science at the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI) in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates. Before joining MBZUAI, he held faculty positions as a Lecturer in the Department of Econometrics and Business Statistics at Monash University in Melbourne, Australia, and as an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Mons, Belgium. He also completed a postdoctoral research fellowship with the Spatio-Temporal Statistics and Data Science group at KAUST in Saudi Arabia. Souhaib earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science (Machine Learning) from Université libre de Bruxelles, Belgium, where he also completed his B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Computer Science. He is a recipient of the Doctoral Research Fellowship from the Belgian National Fund for Scientific Research (FNRS) and the Solvay Award for Best Ph.D. Thesis. His research is supported by multiple competitive grants and has been conducted in collaboration with industry partners including Huawei and John Cockrell. At MBZUAI, he leads the AIM for Scale – AI for Weather Forecasting project, which advances AI-based weather forecasting models and supports their operational deployment in low- and middle-income countries. Souhaib is an Associate Editor for the International Journal of Forecasting and an Area Chair for leading machine learning conferences including NeurIPS, ICML, and AISTATS. He was recognized as a Top Reviewer at ICML and regularly reviews for top-tier journals in machine learning and statistics. He was Local Chair for AISTATS 2026 in Tangier, Morocco. 
 
 
 
  
Professor Salman Khan 
Associate Professor of Computer Vision 
 
Professor Salman Khan is an Associate Professor at the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI), where he leads large-scale initiatives at the intersection of artificial intelligence, climate resilience, and Earth observation. Before joining MBZUAI, he served as a Senior Scientist at the Inception Institute and as a Research Scientist at Data61/CSIRO and received his PhD from The University of Western Australia. His work focuses on building generalizable and scalable AI systems that operate reliably in open-world settings. He works on vision-language models, lifelong and continual learning, and geospatial AI, translating advances in machine learning into real-world applications in climate science, sustainable agriculture, and environmental monitoring. He has authored over 200 publications in leading venues such as CVPR, ICCV, NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, TPAMI, and IJCV, and regularly serves as Area Chair and senior program committee member at major AI conferences. His research has received multiple recognitions, including a CVPR Best Paper nomination, ACCV Best Student Paper Honorable Mention, and the ICML TerraBytes Best Paper Award. 
 
  
Lily U Burns, MA, MSc 
Lily Burns, originally from Rhode Island, USA, is a Senior Advisor for the Undergraduate Program at the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI), a university in Abu Dhabi focused entirely on AI research and education. With over 25 years of experience in higher education, she helps build the university's partnerships and programs around the world.  
Before joining MBZUAI, Lily spent more than 11 years at New York University Abu Dhabi (NYUAD), where she oversaw campus operations, security, and health and safety - including leading the university through the COVID-19 pandemic. Prior to that, she spent a decade at MIT, working with faculty on curriculum, policy, and a joint program between MIT and Harvard Medical School in health sciences. Lily started her career at Harvard Business School and holds two graduate degrees - a Master's in Intercultural Relations and an MSc in International Business Administration. Her work spans operations, crisis management, policy and governance, academic strategy, and international partnerships, with a particular focus on connecting students and institutions on an international level. Lily is also an avid cook, personal trainer, photographer, and mother to a 12-year-old daughter.
